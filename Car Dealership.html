FERRARI’S BEATING HEART: THE V12

The V12 is Ferrari’s most iconic engine, with a history going back to 1947. It also powers the new Purosangue, giving it performance unlike any other car. For 75 years, Ferrari has been making V12 engines that have won world championships, charmed owners, powered the world’s fastest cars, and become the ultimate expression of the internal combustion engine. A V12 powered the first Ferrari and it powers the newest, the game-changing Purosangue. It is Maranello’s most famous engine configuration, a hallmark of so many of its greatest cars.

THE LAMBORGHINI V12 ENGINE

A modern version of the naturally aspirated V12 engine was created for the Aventador in 2011, debuting an enhanced version of the iconic powerplant that maintained the original technological concepts.The Lamborghini Aventador was the last model to feature a pure naturally aspirated V12 engine; full hybridization for the company will commence in the first quarter of 2023. Continue to follow Automobili Lamborghini for a new chapter of automotive history.

Porsche Flat-Six Engine

The Porsche flat-six engine series is a line of mechanically similar, naturally aspirated and sometimes turbocharged, flat-six boxer engines, produced by Porsche for almost 60 consecutive years, since 1963. The engine is an evolution of the flat-four boxer used in the original Volkswagen Beetle.
